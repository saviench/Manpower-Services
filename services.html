Skilled Worker

Workers with special skill, training, knowledge, and ability in their work. A skilled worker may have attended a university, college, or technical school. Alternatively, a skilled worker may have gained their skills on the job. A worker who performs work that necessitates the use of specialized abilities acquired via education. Skilled labor refers to members of the workforce who are highly trained, educated, or experienced and can do more difficult mental or physical activities on the job.

Office Staff / Clerk

Workers who performs clerical office functions, includes filing of document, office machine operations and other similar tasks. The office staff's job also entails operating office machines. Other roles and duties include stenography, word processing and typing, accountancy, and telephone answering.Responsible for performing administrative tasks to support daily business operations.

Hotel Personnel

A worker who ensures and maintains hotel rooms are properly cleaned, disinfected and organized. A hotel housekeeper has received extensive training in the upkeep of a hotel's guest rooms, particularly in terms of general cleanliness. On a normal work day, they conduct the job obligation of carpet cleaning in various hotel rooms as the demand arises. They will also remove rugs from a hotel room and clean them fully, in addition to utilizing expert cleaning gear. Individuals conducting services in the name of the Hotel at the Hotel, regardless of whether they are employed by the Owner, Manager, or an Affiliate of the Owner or Manager, including Senior Executive Personnel.

Maintenance & Utility

Workers who are trained to keep company facilities, repair equipment, regularly inspect projects, and ensures compliance with health and safety regulations. Cleaning and maintaining the company's premises and equipment is the responsibility of utility personnel. Their task is to maintain the upkeep of firm facilities, repair damaged equipment, inspect finished projects, and comply with state health and safety regulations.Maintenance employees, also known as repair workers, restore and maintain mechanical equipment, buildings, and machines.
